Wilmington, North Carolina

His career involved electrical systems, utility infrastructure, safety, reliability, troubleshooting, and the kind of practical problem-solving that comes from years of working with real systems.

That kind of experience does not disappear after retirement.

When someone spends decades around electrical infrastructure, they develop a certain way of thinking. They learn to slow down. They learn to check details. They learn that problems usually have a reason behind them. They also learn that dependable systems are not built by luck. They are built by people who know the work, respect the risks, and take the time to do things correctly.

For Bojekian, that mindset still matters.

He may no longer work full-time in the utilities industry, but the habits from that career continue to show up in daily life. Today, he stays active through student mentorship, community service, basic electronics education, woodworking, amateur radio, home improvement projects, family time, and helping others with practical questions when he can.

His retirement is not about leaving behind the person he was during his career. It is about using that same experience in a different, more personal way.

That kind of work fits naturally with his background.

Many young people know how to operate a device, but they may not understand what is happening behind it.

Bojekian helps make those ideas easier to understand.
